The Akshaya Patra Foundation

At a Glance

Our Mission & Vision

Mission: To provide hot, nutritious, and tasty mid-day meals to children every school day, as an incentive to attend school, by implementing the Government of India's PM POSHAN (formerly Mid-Day Meal) initiative.

Vision: No child in India shall be deprived of education because of hunger.

Key Focus Areas

  • Mid-Day Meal Programme
  • Anganwadi Feeding
  • Food Relief (during crises)
  • Kitchen Operations (Centralised & Decentralised)
  • Public-Private Partnerships
  • Sustainable Development Goals (SDG 2 & SDG 4)

Our Story & Approach

The Akshaya Patra Foundation, established in 2000, began by serving 1,500 children in five government schools in Bengaluru. Today, it serves over 2.35 million children across India.

TAPF operates on a Public-Private Partnership (PPP) model in collaboration with the Government of India and various state governments. Its ISO-certified kitchens and use of technology ensure the safe and efficient preparation of meals at scale.

Our Journey:

  • 2000: Foundation established, serving 1,500 children.
  • 2004: Expanded operations across multiple states.
  • 2015: Achieved milestone of serving 2 billion cumulative meals.
  • 2020: Delivered millions of food relief meals during COVID-19.
  • 2023: Reached over 2.35 million children.

Our Operational Models

  • Centralised Kitchen Model: Large-scale kitchens for urban areas.
  • Decentralised Kitchen Model: Community-based kitchens for remote areas.
  • Food Safety & Quality Control: Stringent hygiene standards.
  • Partnerships & Funding: Supported by corporates and government.
