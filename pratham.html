Pratham Foundation

At a Glance

Our Mission & Vision

Mission: Pratham's primary goal is to solve the problem of low learning levels in India's schools, ensuring that children don't just attend school but acquire the fundamental skills necessary to succeed.

Vision: “Our vision is to end the cycle of poverty in India by providing access to education.”

Key Features

  • Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN): To ensure all children (6-14 years) can read basic text and solve basic arithmetic.
  • "Teaching at the Right Level" (TaRL): An innovative methodology where children are grouped by ability, not grade, to catch up quickly.
  • Research, Assessment, and Advocacy (ASER): A citizen-led household survey that assesses foundational learning levels in rural India.
  • Vocational Training (Pratham Institute): Provides employable skills in high-growth fields to economically disadvantaged youth (18-25).
  • Early Childhood Education: Running play-based pre-schools (balwadis) for children aged 3-6 to make them "ready for school."
  • "Second Chance" Program: Provides female dropouts an opportunity to complete their secondary school education (Grade 10).

Our Story & Approach

Pratham’s approach is characterized by high-quality, low-cost, and easily replicable interventions focused on addressing the foundational learning crisis in India and globally. Its core strategy centers on two main pillars: a unique pedagogical method and a model for large-scale systemic impact.

The Pratham NGO was co-founded by Dr. Madhav Chavan and Farida Lambay.

Our Operational Models

  • The Diagnostic Tool (ASER): Diagnosing the problem through the Annual Status of Education Report (ASER), the largest citizen-led survey in India.
  • The Core Intervention (TaRL): Using the "Teaching at the Right Level" methodology.
  • The Scaling Strategy: Using partnerships and low-cost models to scale.

Our Journey

  • 1995: Universal Access (Mumbai)
  • 1998-2002: Scaling Remedial Work
  • 2005: Measuring Learning Crisis and Youth Skilling
  • 2007–2009: Rapid Catch-up
  • 2010–2012: Methodology Refinement
  • 2011: Secondary Education
  • 2014–Present: Scaling via Government
  • 2020–Present: Digital & Policy Alignment

Pratham’s guiding principle and overall call to action is: “Every Child in School and Learning Well”
